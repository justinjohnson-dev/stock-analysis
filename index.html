Company Overview

Business Profile

Nike, Inc. is the world's largest athletic footwear and apparel brand, with key categories including basketball, running, and football (soccer). The company maintains a dominant global presence in the sportswear market through its iconic brands that include Nike, Jordan, and Converse.

Footwear generates approximately two-thirds of Nike's sales, with the remaining revenue coming from apparel and equipment. The company has a robust global distribution network spanning over 40 countries through company-owned stores, franchised locations, third-party retailers, and e-commerce platforms.

Nike's business model relies heavily on outsourcing production, with nearly all manufacturing handled by contract manufacturers in more than 30 countries. This approach has historically allowed Nike to maintain flexibility and focus on its core competencies of design, marketing, and distribution, though recent tariff issues are challenging this model.

The primary factor weighing on Nike's stock is the immediate threat of tariffs, particularly on Vietnam, where the company manufactures approximately 50% of its footwear. The recently announced 10% blanket tariff on global imports creates substantial uncertainty around Nike's cost structure and profitability.

However, several positive factors prevent a Sell recommendation. Nike's core brand strength remains intact, notable investors like Bill Ackman continue to accumulate shares, and the new CEO is implementing a focused turnaround strategy. Additionally, there are emerging catalysts including Vietnam's interest in negotiating tariff reductions, strategic partnerships like the NikeSKIMS collaboration, and a dividend yield at 15-year highs.
